The buying process, step by step

  1. Sign a Buyer Representation Agreement

    A Buyer-Broker Representation Agreement is a written contract that clearly outlines the services I provide, how long our agreement lasts, and how compensation works. It protects you by making everything transparent before we do anything together. California law (AB 2992, effective January 2025) requires a signed agreement before we tour any property. We review it together before you sign anything, and I'll answer every question you have.

  2. Get pre-approved

    Before we search seriously, you need a pre-approval letter so we know your real budget and can move fast when the right home appears. I can connect you with trusted lenders in the area if you need a referral.

  3. Define your priorities

    We'll have a real conversation about must-haves versus nice-to-haves, target neighborhoods, timeline, and budget. I'll give you honest input on tradeoffs and what to realistically expect in this market.

  4. Search with intention

    I'll send you curated listings based on what we actually talked about. You can also search live on your own any time using my home search tool below.

  5. Tour and evaluate

    I'll be at every showing, asking the questions you might not think to ask, and giving you honest feedback on each property. My marketing background means I also know how homes are staged and priced to sell, which helps you see past the presentation.

  6. Make a strong offer

    How your offer is written matters in a competitive market. We'll craft something compelling, position you well, and make sure the terms work in your favor wherever possible.

  7. Navigate escrow and close

    Inspections, contingencies, disclosures, paperwork: I'll walk you through all of it and make sure nothing falls through the cracks between acceptance and close.
