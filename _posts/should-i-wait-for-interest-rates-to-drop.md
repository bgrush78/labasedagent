**Stop waiting for a rate that might never come.**

If I had a dollar for every conversation that started with "we're just going to wait until rates drop," I could probably cover a down payment on a duplex in Signal Hill.

I get it. Nobody wants to feel like they're overpaying on financing. But here's the truth I've been telling clients all year: waiting for mortgage rates to crash back to 2021 levels isn't a strategy. It's a hope. And hope doesn't get you a house in a market like Southern California, where good inventory doesn't sit around waiting for you to feel ready.

## The Sidelines Are Getting Crowded

Rates may come down eventually. They also might not — not to the levels people are picturing, anyway. Meanwhile, every month spent waiting is a month of rent paid to someone else's mortgage, a month of appreciation you didn't capture, and a month where the seller across the table is getting more, not less, motivated to make a deal work.

That last part is the piece a lot of buyers miss. In this rate environment, sellers know exactly why buyers are hesitant. That gives us leverage right now that disappears the second rates drop and buyer demand floods back in. If rates fall significantly, you're not just competing with today's buyers — you're competing with everyone who's been sitting on the sidelines with you.

## What You Can Actually Control

The advertised rate is one input in a much bigger equation. Here's where the real work happens:

- **Seller concessions.** In the right situation, we can negotiate the seller into covering closing costs or funding a temporary rate buydown — meaning you start out at a lower effective rate for the first year or two, giving you breathing room while you settle in.
- **The right loan for your actual situation.** FHA, VA, conventional, jumbo, non-QM — these aren't one-size-fits-all, and the "best" rate on paper isn't always the best product for your goals. Part of my job is running the comparison so you're not guessing.
- **Down payment assistance and grants.** There's more of this available than most buyers realize, and it can meaningfully change what "getting in" looks like for you.
- **Buy now, refinance later.** "Marry the house, date the rate" gets thrown around a lot, but there's real logic behind it. You lock in the property and the price today, and if rates improve down the road, you refinance into them. You're not stuck — you're positioned.
- **Financing built around your life, not a headline number.** Structuring the loan around your actual budget and long-term plans matters more than chasing a number you saw in a headline.

## Replace Rate Anxiety With a Plan

The buyers who move forward right now aren't the ones who stopped caring about rates. They're the ones who stopped letting rate anxiety make the decision for them. Once you can see the full picture — concessions, loan structure, assistance programs, refinance timeline — the advertised rate stops feeling like a locked gate and starts looking like just one line item you can work around.
