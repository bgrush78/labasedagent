Real estate is full of things nobody explains clearly. Here are the ones I hear most often from buyers and sellers in Los Angeles and beyond.

A Buyer-Broker Representation Agreement is a written contract between you and your agent that clearly outlines the services your agent will provide, how long the agreement lasts, and how your agent will be compensated. It exists to protect you by making everything transparent before we do anything together. As of January 1, 2025, California law (AB 2992) requires a signed Buyer-Broker Representation Agreement before your agent can tour homes with you, so yes, you will need to sign one. We'll go through it together before you sign anything, and I'll answer every question you have.

The way buyer agent compensation works has changed significantly. Your agent's fee is now negotiated directly between you and your agent, not set by the seller. In many transactions, you can ask the seller to cover some or all of your agent's compensation as a concession, which they may accept or decline. If they decline, you remain responsible for the agreed amount. We'll discuss all of this clearly in our first conversation so there are no surprises at any point in the process.
